Description

Jollof rice is rich and flavorful West African dish made with long-grain rice cooked in a savory tomato sauce blended with peppers,Onions, and aromatic spices.

Slowed cooked to perfection, the rice absorbs the deep flavors of the seasoned stew, giving it a vibrant red color and irrestible taste, often served with a grilled chicken, fried plantains, or salad, Jollof rice is a celebratory dish enjoyed at parties, family gatherings, and special occasions

Ingredients

  • Long grain parboiled rice
  • Plum tomatoes
  • Red bell peppers
  • Scotch bonnet peppers
  • Vegetable oil
  • Tomato paste
  • Chicken stock or broth
  • Curry powder
  • Dried thyme
  • Bay leaves
  • Salt and Maggi

Steps

  1. Prepare the base: Blend the tomatoes, red bell peppers, scotch bonnets until smooth.
  2. Saute the Aromatics: Heat vegetable oil in a large pot and fry sliced onions until they are soft and golden.
  3. Fry the Paste: Add the tomato paste to the onions and fry for several minutes to deepen the flavor and remove the tanginess.
  4. Cook the Sauce: Pour in the blended pepper mix. Cook it down until the water evaporates and the oil starts to rise to the top.
  5. Season: Stir in chicken stock, curry powder, thyme, bay leaves, salt, and Maggi cubes. Bring everything to a boil.
  6. Add the rice: Wash your parboiled rice thoroughly, add to the pot, and stir. Ensure the liquid is hjust slightly above the rice level.
  7. Steam: Cover the pot slightly. Cook on the low heat until the rice is tender and the liquid is fully absorbed.
  8. The "Smoky" Finish: Once done, turn up the heat for the last minute to slightly burn the bottom for that classy party-style smoky flavor.
