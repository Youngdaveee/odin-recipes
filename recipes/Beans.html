Description

Nigerian Honey Beans also known as Oloyin, are famous for their unique naturally sweet taste. These beansare slow-cooked until they reach a creamy and buttery, consistency that melts in your mouth.

Whether you enjoy them plain, with a side of fried plantain, or soaked with garri, this dish is a protein-packed staple that brings comfort to every meal.

Ingredients

  • Honey beans(Oloyin)
  • Palm Oil
  • Ground Crayfish
  • Scotch Bonnet
  • Salt and Seasoning
  • Water

Steps

  1. Boil and Rinse: Place the beans in a pot with water, bring to boil for about 10 minutes, then drain and rinse the beans to help remove excess gas
  2. Slow Cook: Return the beans to the pot with fresh water and the chopped onions. Cook on medium-low head until the beans are very soft and tender
  3. Season: Add the palm oil, ground crayfish, blended peppers, and seasoning cubes
  4. Mash and Stimmer: Stir everything together, slightly mashing some of the beans with your spoon to create a thick, creamy sauce. Let it simmer for another 10 minutes on low heat
